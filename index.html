Investment Thesis

Workforce housing in supply-constrained submarkets, bought below replacement cost.

The Inland Empire is among the most structurally undersupplied rental markets in the country. Population and logistics-driven employment continue to grow across Ontario, Fontana, Rancho Cucamonga, and Riverside, while new multifamily construction remains concentrated in luxury product that the regional workforce cannot absorb. The result is durable, deepening demand for well-managed B and C-class apartments.

We acquire underperforming properties in the 10-to-20 unit range — a segment too small for institutional buyers and too operationally demanding for most private owners. These assets routinely trade with below-market rents, deferred maintenance, and inefficient operations: precisely the conditions where disciplined ownership creates value independent of market direction.

Every acquisition is underwritten to a forced-appreciation outcome. Value is created at the property level — through renovation, revenue optimization, and expense control — and measured as the spread between our stabilized yield on cost and prevailing market capitalization rates.

Strategy

Value is created in execution, not acquisition.

Our process is built to be repeatable: source off-market, underwrite conservatively, execute the business plan with in-house oversight, and manage for long-term performance.

01

Source

We originate opportunities through direct-to-owner outreach, broker relationships cultivated within our four core submarkets, and lender networks surfacing distressed or fatigued ownership. Our size segment sits below the institutional radar, which keeps competition rational and pricing negotiable.

02

Underwrite

Every deal is modeled from the unit level up — current rents against verified market comps, line-item renovation budgets, and conservative exit assumptions. We stress-test debt service against rate movement and vacancy before a letter of intent is ever issued. If the basis doesn't work, we pass.

03

Execute

Business plans are executed with direct principal oversight rather than delegated to third parties. Renovation scopes are sequenced to minimize vacancy drag, contractors are managed on-site, and revenue initiatives — rent normalization, utility recapture, ancillary income — are implemented in the first ownership year.

04

Manage

Stabilized assets are run as operating businesses: monthly financial review against underwriting, preventative maintenance programs that protect basis, and resident retention that keeps economic occupancy high. We refinance at stabilization to return capital and hold for durable cash flow.
